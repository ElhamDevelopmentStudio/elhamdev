UX/UI

User Experience (UX): UX focuses on the overall feel and usability of a product from the user's perspective. It encompasses a user's emotions, perceptions, and interactions with the product, aiming to create a positive and satisfying experience. UX design involves research, usability testing, information architecture, and wireframing to understand user needs and preferences. The goal of UX design is to make a product intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable, ultimately leading to higher user satisfaction and engagement. User Interface (UI): UI design is a subset of UX design and deals with the visual elements and layout of a product. It focuses on the aesthetics and interactive elements that users directly interact with, such as buttons, menus, and navigation. UI designers create visually appealing and consistent designs that align with the product's brand and user expectations. UI design also involves prototyping, creating mockups, and working closely with developers to implement the design effectively.
